squares style guide

What follows below are the primary building blocks. To maintain interest over the open-ended life of this series, at one point or another each of these 'rules' is expected to be broken. In order to maintain a consistent look and feel, though, the majority of elements throughout the series should hew to these guidelines.

colors

The observant reader will already have noticed that this system gets ignored as soon as the third squares page. The majority of pages, though, should incorporate deep, rich, so-called 'jewel-tone' colors. Backgrounds: Black, to make the foreground colors pop, except where the background of the page is the primary focus.

fonts

Since this was never intended to be a copy-heavy exercise, two weights of a single font is all that is required. More weights would be a nice-to-have, but restricting it to two helps keep the page load time down. As for "Why Cairo?", it is a surprisingly readable and perky font in spite of its squareish overall characteristics.

text styles

You can futz with the colors, you can fart around with the background, but you'd better have a damn good reason to fuck up the type hierarchy. It is the primary constant on each page (aside from the placement of the squares themselves). The only variation needed so far has been to dump an 85% opacity black background underneath it when the background isn't black.

copy in squares (<h1>)

  • font-size: 5em
  • font-weight: 200 (light)
  • color: white

kicker (td.top)

  • font-size: .85em
  • font-weight: 700 (bold)
  • color: dimgray
  • letter-spacing: .2em
  • border-bottom: 2px solid dimgray

headline (td.middle)

  • font-size: 3em
  • font-weight: 200 (light)
  • color: darkgray
  • letter-spacing: .01em
  • line-height: 1.25em

body copy (td.bottom)

  • font-size: 1em
  • font-weight: 200 (light)
  • color: darkgray
  • letter-spacing: .05em
  • line-height: 1.75em

The genesis for this series was to try to finally figure out how element positioning worked in CSS. (As of this writing, after completing "squares010", I'm still not there.) After the first couple of pages, though, it's become a test bed for other HTML and CSS learnings and discoveries.

The two constants throughout have been the copy style and positioning (fixed at the bottom), and the size and position of the three squares themselves. As with the copy treatment, there better be a damn good reason for altering the squares' placement, as there was for squares003.
